Discription :
A Normal Motherhood is an acrylic painting created in my signature Uncurvism style a visual language where the central subjects are intentionally built without curves, using only straight lines and geometric forms.

We call it normal because society has made it look ordinary.

But behind every ordinary day is a woman carrying far more than a child.

In this painting, every element tells a story.

The gun is not a symbol of violence. It represents a mother's instinct to protect to become fearless when her child needs safety.

The white sari represents the loneliness many women experience after childbirth. While every family is different, many mothers carry most of the physical and emotional responsibilities, often feeling as though they're raising a child alone.

The kitchen isn't just a room. It's where countless mothers spend much of their day, preparing meals while quietly putting everyone else's needs before their own.

This painting isn't about tragedy.

It's about the sacrifices we've normalized.

Sometimes, the strongest person in a home isn't the loudest one.

Uncuvism — where reality is drawn in straight lines, and illusion lives in curves.
